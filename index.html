Ika

This animation was created as part of a university project and represents my first major work in 3D animation. It follows a scientist named Ika, who is excited about a portal he has built. He rides his hoverboard around the dining table, waves, then heads through the hallway into the garage, where he accidentally falls over the railing. After getting back up, he jumps into the portal and disappears.

The animation was originally imagined as a backstory for a game I was planning to develop. While working on it, I discovered how passionate I am about animation. This project marked the moment I realized I wanted to focus more seriously on animation and modeling. I created the entire project myself - from modeling and environment design to animation and rendering.

Mars Escape

Mars Escape was my first contact with game development – a 2D platformer created as a demo project for the Introduction to Video Games course at university. In the game, the player explores the surface of Mars, collecting coins and avoiding aliens that patrol the platforms and can kill the player on contact.

The game was developed in Unity using the Bolt visual scripting tool, which was a requirement for the course assignment. I created all the visual assets myself in Photoshop. I chose a pixel art style because it allowed me to achieve a cohesive and appealing look, even without advanced drawing skills.

I edited all sound effects in Audacity, tailoring them to match the visual style and overall atmosphere of the game.

Mars Escape gave me a solid introduction to the game development process and laid the foundation for future projects.

Way Out

Way Out is a 2D pixel art puzzle game with escape room elements, developed for university using OpenGL. The game consists of three levels. In the first two, the player must find a key by solving logical puzzles — like moving books, chess pieces, and interpreting hidden messages. The third level presents another puzzle that must be solved in order to choose the correct door and escape.

All visual assets were created in Photoshop using a pixel art style, and sound effects were edited in Audacity. The demo version of the game is completed and represents my take on classic escape room mechanics within a 2D environment.

Exit

Exit is a top-down RPG demo in which the player takes on the role of Petar Venčić – a man who, after clinical death, wakes up buried in his own grave and manages to dig himself out. He doesn’t know how he survived, but he knows he must find a way out of the eerie cemetery. As he wanders through the graveyard, he soon realizes he is not alone – a group of cultists is there to perform a ritual, and Petar becomes their target.

In order to survive and escape, Petar finds an improvised weapon and begins searching for the key that unlocks the cemetery gate. His path leads him to a chapel, where he faces the most dangerous confrontation with those who want him dead.

The game features an intro sequence made up of images I drew, accompanied by typewriter-style text, creating an atmospheric introduction to the story and hinting at the mysterious background of the events.

Exit was created as part of a university course, using Unity, Photoshop, Maya, and Audacity.

Moon Defense - The Zarax Menace

Moon Defense - The Zarax Menace is a 3D tower defense strategy set in the year 2300 on the Moon, where the player takes on the role of a commander tasked with defending Luna Link, the main communication tower between the Moon and Earth. Players must use their strategic skills to defend the tower from mysterious alien ships known as Zarax.

Enemies emerge from tunnels and move using the A* algorithm, making each wave increasingly challenging. As the rounds progress, more enemies appear, and an additional challenge comes from the UFO, which randomly spawns new enemies at different points along the path.

Since the game was developed for a university project, the focus was on allowing the player to interact with the environment (placing, removing, and upgrading objects...). In addition to these and basic mechanics, artificial intelligence elements were implemented, including the A* algorithm for enemy navigation, state machine systems for their behavior, and additional AI logic that adds complexity and dynamism to the game.

The game was developed using Unity, Maya, Photoshop, and Audacity.
